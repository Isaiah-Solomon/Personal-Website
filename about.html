About

A little more about me.

I grew up taking things apart to see how they worked, and never really stopped — I just traded screwdrivers for a text editor. These days that curiosity goes into interfaces: how a product feels in someone's hands, and what it takes, technically, to make that feeling hold up.

I studied Computer Science before moving into design, which means I'm equally comfortable sketching a flow on paper and opening dev tools to see why a transition is janky. I've spent the last seven years moving between design and engineering teams — usually as the person translating between them.

Outside of work, I read more nonfiction than I finish, run slowly and without complaint, and I'm slowly restoring an old turntable that mostly still doesn't work.
